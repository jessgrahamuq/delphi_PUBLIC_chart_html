4.1 Disinformation, surveillance, and influence at scale - Optional Actors Responsibility Assessment

Higher Responsibility

Summary of expert comments: Comments emphasized specialized developers' high responsibility in sensitive domains like media, intelligence, and politics where misuse directly affects democratic stability, with tools potentially empowering malicious use even when open-sourced.

See all expert comments (3)
  • "Specialized developers are highly responsible in domains like media, intelligence, and politics, where misuse directly affects democratic stability."
  • "• AI Developer (General-purpose and Specialized): These actors hold direct control over models capable of language manipulation, data harvesting, and automated influence at scale. Their tools may unintentionally empower malicious use even without direct intent, especially when open-sourced or made publicly available."
  • "... developers are most responsible for providing the tools to enable these risks and subsequent harms."

Lower Responsibility

Summary of expert comments: Some comments noted specialized developers have lower responsibility as specialized models pose less risk than general models, though context remains important.

See all expert comments (2)
  • "While developers of all kinds should be highly responsible, I chose not to make them primarily responsible because the use of a model to produce disinformation, the choice to use targeted data poisoning to implant disinformation into a model, and other factors may be the primary factor leading to the spread of disinformation or the use of a model for influence and surveillance. It will depend on the context."
  • "AI Developer (Specialized AI): Liability is moderate, as specialized AI can be used in unforeseen ways, but the risk is lower compared to general models"

Higher Responsibility

Summary of expert comments: Comments varied, with most noting moderate responsibility for platforms potentially exploited for mass surveillance, while backend systems can silently empower disinformation operations if poorly governed.

See all expert comments (3)
  • "Infrastructure providers have moderate responsibility, since their platforms can be exploited for mass surveillance."
  • "• AI Infrastructure Provider: While not directly involved in content generation, their backend systems (e.g., cloud platforms, compute services, data centers) enable scalability and automation. If compromised or poorly governed, they can silently empower widespread disinformation operations."
  • "If we conceptualize the 'information space' as a public good, we can identify inherent collective action problems that will worsen with AI-enabled disinformation. Typically, a central, legitimate governing authority is needed to resolve these issues, suggesting AI Governance actors may necessarily be primary responsibility holders. However, increasingly the central governing authority may become the AI developer/deployer/infrastructure all in one (for example, Meta and disinformation on Facebook)"

Lower Responsibility

One commenter said: "AI Infrastructure Provider: Liability is limited on a technical level. It does not directly influence the purpose of using AI."

Higher Responsibility

No comments provided.

Lower Responsibility

Summary of expert comments: Comments consistently characterized affected stakeholders as not responsible, being victims and targets of manipulation rather than actors, including marginalized populations with minimal power to counter AI influence.

See all expert comments (2)
  • "Users and affected stakeholders should not be held responsible, as they are the main targets."
  • "Affected Stakeholder: It is more of a victim of the use of AI for influence purposes, not a responsible actor."
